As per Graham,

> Upon analysis' provides safety of principal and an adequate return

The key here is **analysis**. You need to imagine buying stocks like you are buying a house: you will analyze the location, neighbors, costs, etc. The same should be done for stocks. Since **you are not trading**, like when you buy an house you plan to stay.

In this regard, **investment is different from speculating**, even though speculation is inherent to common stock commitments. What you need to achieve is keeping it controlled by avoiding:
1. speculating when you think you are investing
2. speculating seriously instead of a past-time
3. risking more money that you can afford to lose
If you really want to speculate, put aside a portion dedicated to it and **never put more money**.
