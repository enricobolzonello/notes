> [!Definition]
> In psychology, the Zeigarnik effect occurs when an activity that has been interrupted may be more readily recalled. It postulates that people remember unfinished or interrupted tasks better than completed tasks.
>  — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zeigarnik_effect

There are two ways to interpret it:
1) unfinished tasks occupy short-term memory. Since we can remember maximum 7 things (to add reference), closing tasks or postponing in a meaningful way (like taking a note) frees us from cognitive load
2) suspending study to perform unrelated activities will remember materials better
