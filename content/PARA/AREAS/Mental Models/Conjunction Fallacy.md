# [[2. Mental Models]] 
# Overview

> [!Definition]
> False belief that a conjunction of two events is more probable than one of the two events on its own

The longer version of the experiment separated the two critical elements with an intermediate element, so readers would treat every element as separated. In the shorter version there was a direct comparison between the two critical elements so most of the readers with statistics knowledge would not fall in the fallacy.
